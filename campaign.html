Hannibal's Campaign

Hannibal’s campaign in the Second Punic War remains one of history's boldest military feats. Determined to challenge Rome, he led his army from Spain, across the Alps, and into Italy, overcoming immense odds and heavy losses. Once in Italy, he achieved major victories at Trebia, Lake Trasimene, and Cannae, where his tactical brilliance shocked Rome and left a lasting mark on military strategy. For over a decade, Hannibal maintained his army in Italy, rallying local allies and avoiding Roman defeat despite limited support from Carthage. Eventually, however, Rome’s resilience and strategic pressure on Carthage forced Hannibal to return home, where he was defeated by Scipio Africanus at the Battle of Zama in 202 BCE, ending Carthage’s challenge to Rome. His campaign transformed the art of war and is remembered for its innovation and boldness.

Crossing the Alps

To begin his conquest Hannibal lead his troops through the Alps to avoid Rome’s naval strength. It is regarded as one of the greatest achievements of ancient warfare.

Battle of Ticinus

Hannibal faced off against his rival Publius Cornelius “Africanus” Scipio. The Carthaginians prevailed outnumbering the opposing forces ~6,000 to ~3,600. The battle took place next to the Ticino River.

Battle of Trebia

Hannibal’s first major victory came in this battle along the Trebia river near modern Piacenza. About 21,000 Carthaginian soldiers caused around 30,000 Roman casualties.

Battle of Trasimene

A major victory for Hannibal, he lead an ambush against the Roman forces. The Romans fled, losing about 15,000 soldiers. It is considered one of the largest ambushes in history. It took place on the shore of Lake Trasimene.

Battle of Cannae

A major win for the Carthaginians with losses for the Romans from 30,000 to 55,000. Hannibal slowly surrounded and slaughtered the Romans by placing his weaker forces in the middle. This is considered the last major battle that Hannibal fought in Rome.

Battle of Zama

Hannibal and his troops exited Rome from Croton in order to defend Carthage. Ultimately Hannibal was defeated by his rival Scipio Africanus in Zama. Afterwards, Carthage surrendered, lost Spain, and was forced to pay a large sum to Rome
